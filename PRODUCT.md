# SigOS — Product Context

## Product

SigOS is the internal operating system for Signature Cleans, a commercial cleaning company scaling toward a £20M franchise model. It is a private, authenticated web app used by the leadership team and virtual assistants to run daily operations.

## Register

**product** — design serves the task. This is a tool, not a brand surface.

## Users

| Role | Who | How they use it |
|------|-----|-----------------|
| admin | Nelson (MD/COO) | Oversight, financials, operatives, all modules |
| sales | Nick (CEO) | CRM, pipeline, deals, quotes, cold calling |
| operations | Ops staff | Operatives, contract health, growth |
| va | Virtual assistants | Tasks, leads, hub apps |

## Tone

Professional without being corporate. Functional without being spartan. The interface should disappear into the work — users are moving fast, making decisions, not admiring the UI.

## Brand

- Primary: `#2056A4` (brand blue — actions, links, primary state)
- Accent: `#7DB227` (brand green — ops/growth indicators)
- Logo: circular badge (`/logo-badge.jpg`)
- Company name: Signature Cleans

## Anti-references

- Bloated enterprise SaaS (Salesforce, SAP) — dense, corporate, overwhelming
- Startup vanity dashboards — big hero numbers, gradient accents, flashy but hollow
- Generic admin templates — no personality, identical to a thousand other tools

## Strategic intent

Every screen should feel like Linear or Stripe's dashboard: fast, dense when needed, trustworthy, never decorated for its own sake.

## Physical scene

Nelson at his desk in Exeter, 9am, making fast operational decisions across shifts, contracts, and pipeline. The interface should surface information quickly and get out of the way. No drama.
